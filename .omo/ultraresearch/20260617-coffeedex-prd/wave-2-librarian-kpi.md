# Wave 2: KPI and Measurement

## Findings

- Revenue is an outcome, not the product value unit. A better provisional North Star is weekly validated coffee memories: user-confirmed cards that persist and contribute to a useful archive.
- Activation should be first durable/validated card, not signup. Retention should follow coffee cadence: second distinct bag and archive revisit, not generic D1 opens.
- Scan success requires capture completion, per-field accuracy, critical-card correctness, correction burden and latency. Edit-free acceptance is behavioral, not ground truth.
- A share-button click rate is not a viral coefficient. Measure artifact generation, actual measurable public views and referred activation; native share APIs often expose intent rather than confirmed posting.
- Public cross-industry benchmarks are context only and cannot become CoffeeDex launch thresholds.

## EXPAND markers

- LEAD: validated-card event contract — WHY: required to measure value and quality — ANGLE: event taxonomy and reconciliation.
- LEAD: scan target decomposition — WHY: current accuracy language is ambiguous — ANGLE: field precision/recall, critical-card exact match, correction time and strata.
- DEAD END: treating a 5% share click rate as viral growth.
- DEAD END: universal retention or paid-conversion benchmarks.

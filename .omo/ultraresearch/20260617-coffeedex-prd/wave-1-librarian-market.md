# Wave 1: Market, JTBD, Visual Collection

## Findings

- Coffee logging is split into brew utilities (Beanconqueror, Filtru), journals/community (Tasting Grounds), and visual/AI memory (BeanBook). BeanBook already markets bag scanning, a visual shelf, tasting comparison, and annual insights, making CoffeeDex's stated feature set a direct competitor rather than a unique category.
- Coffee-specific community evidence repeatedly frames the job as remembering bags, retrieving liked coffees, and reducing physical clutter. Social sharing appears secondary.
- Photo capture is a credible low-friction behavior, while Notion/spreadsheet flows are repeatedly described as too cumbersome on mobile. Evidence is mostly self-selected enthusiast discussion and must not be treated as prevalence data.
- Transferable collection patterns are shelf/wardrobe metaphors, owned vs want-to-try states, diary/check-ins, curated identity profiles, search/filter, and selective share artifacts.

## EXPAND markers

- LEAD: app-review complaints about quick add, search, export and photo support — WHY: validates actual gaps beyond product copy — ANGLE: App Store/Play review corpus.
- LEAD: BeanBook differentiation and traction — WHY: closest direct competitor — ANGLE: onboarding, reviews, pricing, scan limits and report depth.
- LEAD: searchable memory may outrank social sharing — WHY: changes positioning and MVP — ANGLE: user interviews/review corpus.
- LEAD: owned vs want-to-try state — WHY: may improve recurring utility before purchase — ANGLE: test against tight MVP scope.
- DEAD END: no strong evidence that users primarily want a coffee social network.

# Wave 1: Monetization, Korea, Onboarding, Taste

## Findings

- Lifetime unlimited AI is structurally risky because the price is fixed while inference/support costs recur. Hybrid recurring or credit models are common, but broad consumer subscription benchmarks do not prove willingness-to-pay for CoffeeDex.
- Korea-specific evidence supports Naver/community discovery, local checkout expectations, and explicit consent; there is no clean official measure of specialty-coffee app demand.
- Guest-first, system photo picker, permission at point of use, and progressive authentication are well-supported platform/design patterns. Three-photo import is a hypothesis, not a proven optimum; recommend 3 but allow 1.
- A personal taste passport is defensible as a descriptive summary of the user's own ratings. Origin/process must not be presented causally, and radar charts can distort comparisons. The trained-panel literature is context, not a requirement for a personal preference product.

# Expansion Log

Session: 20260615-hyangmi-brand-redesign

## Phase 0

Core question: Hyangmi/CoffeeDex needs a stronger brand and a less generic, less AI-looking web experience. Research comparable apps/websites and define a brand + frontend redesign direction that can be implemented in the current Next app.

Axes:
- Codebase visual system: current landing, dashboard, pricing, global CSS, design tokens, and high-impact redesign entry points.
- Conversion surfaces: payment, pricing, support, auth, public share, onboarding, and tests that constrain redesign.
- Competitor/adjacent products: coffee journals, tasting apps, subscriptions, roaster discovery, flavor archive products.
- Specialty coffee visual identity: roaster/editorial coffee websites, packaging language, typography, photography, texture.
- Anti-generic AI UI: AI products that feel artifact-first, domain-specific, and trustworthy rather than gradient/SaaS generic.

Codebase relevant: yes. External: yes. Browsing: yes. Verification likely: yes. Report requested: no, but synthesis and implementation direction required.

## Expansion Decision

Leads converge into the same practical implementation cluster:
- landing hero and product artifact framing,
- pricing/paywall hierarchy,
- dashboard/conversion surfaces later,
- story/public-card artifact system later,
- global visual tokens.

Closed as sufficient for this implementation pass:
- Detailed competitor catalog expansion: enough to establish brand direction.
- Additional AI product expansion: enough to establish artifact-first pattern.
- Full dashboard redesign: deferred because user complaint is primarily web/brand first impression and a broad dashboard rewrite would be a separate pass.

Implementation focus:
- Redesign the public landing and pricing surfaces first, because they are the brand’s first viewport and most visible web surface.

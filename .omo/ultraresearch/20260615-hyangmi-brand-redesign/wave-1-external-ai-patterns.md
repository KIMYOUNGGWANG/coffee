## Key Findings

- Strong non-generic AI products do not sell AI first. They sell a tangible artifact, ritual, or workflow first.
- AI is framed as embedded capability: search, summarize, connect, annotate, explain, or retrieve from the user’s artifact set.
- Trust comes from visible provenance, constrained scope, rich object models, and output surfaces.
- For Hyangmi, AI should appear as label scan, note refinement, flavor clustering, provenance/confidence, and searchable archive support, not as generic “AI assistant” branding.

## Key Findings

- The coffee industry has a shared sensory vocabulary, but the SCA guide frames it as intuitive and iterative: start broad, then move outward to specificity.
- WCR Sensory Lexicon is a standardized research instrument with 110 flavor, aroma, and texture attributes plus intensity references.
- Beginner coffee education explicitly supports terms such as fragrance, aroma, flavor, aftertaste, acidity, sweetness, and mouthfeel.
- Modern sensory education tries to escape jargon and support more intuitive language.
- Korea has an existing cupping education community, but broad cupping literacy still benefits from beginner-first Korean scaffolding.
- Habit and self-tracking research supports brief repeated logging, reflection, and low-burden capture.

## Product Implications

- Use center-out guided taste selection, e.g. fruity -> citrus -> lemon, rather than empty free text first.
- Add beginner Korean glossary cards and constrained choices before advanced notes.
- Let users capture with one tap, voice, photo, and optional later detail.
- Add compare-with-last-cup prompts to turn archive into learning.
- Treat streaks as secondary; immediate usefulness and reduced intimidation should drive retention.

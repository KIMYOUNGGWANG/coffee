# Ultraresearch Expansion Log

Core question: Hyangmi, a Korea-first specialty-coffee memory product, should move in which product direction based on codebase truth, community demand, competitor signals, and market willingness to pay.

Axes:
- Product truth: shipped capabilities, promised capabilities, Korean-first copy, checkout/entitlement surface, and docs boundaries in the dex repo.
- Korean communities: Naver/blog/forum/cafe-like public posts, Korean search surfaces, and local specialty-coffee discussion around tasting notes, bean buying, brewing, and cafe discovery.
- Global communities: Reddit, Home-Barista, CoffeeGeek, Hacker News/Product Hunt where coffee tracking, bean logs, grinder/recipe tooling, and consumer coffee apps are discussed.
- Competitors and substitutes: Beanconqueror, Filtru, Aeromatic, coffee-tasting notebooks, Notion templates, bean subscription/cafe discovery products, and roaster-side tools.
- Market and monetization: specialty coffee growth, consumer willingness to pay, retention loops, subscriptions, marketplace/referral risks, and B2B/B2C wedges.
- Product strategy synthesis: defensible Korea-first positioning, near-term roadmap, anti-directions, and evidence-backed bets.

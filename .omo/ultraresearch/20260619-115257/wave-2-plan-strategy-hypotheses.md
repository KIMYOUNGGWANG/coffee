## Hypotheses Ranked

1. Fresh Shelf + Rebuy Timing
   - Target: Korean home-cafe drinker buying 200g bags every 1-4 weeks.
   - Pain: forgets roast date, peak window, grams left, and what to rebuy.
   - Wedge: private bean shelf that says drink now, wait, finish soon, rebuy.
   - First experiment: 30 users, 14 days, manual photo intake plus freshness/rebuy reminders.

2. No-App Coffee Memory
   - Target: coffee buyers who reject heavy logging apps.
   - Pain: apps and Notion/spreadsheets are slow on mobile.
   - Wedge: send a bag photo, get a remembered coffee.
   - First experiment: landing/upload flow with usable memory under 30 seconds.

3. Korean Taste Compass
   - Target: beginner-to-intermediate specialty buyers.
   - Pain: roaster notes do not translate into confident purchase decisions.
   - Wedge: plain Korean taste explanations tied to personal memory.
   - First experiment: compare roaster notes only vs. Hyangmi plain-Korean taste card.

4. One-Cup Dial-In Coach
   - Useful but should follow capture retention; otherwise risks becoming a heavy enthusiast tool.

5. Subscription Cadence Guard
   - Narrow test for existing subscription users; not broad positioning.

6. Marketplace-Free Roaster Watchlist
   - Promising but dangerous; keep as saved links and intent, no checkout.

7. Guided Home Tasting Flights
   - Good education layer, not core wedge.

## Key Findings

- Market splits into deep brew logging/hardware, memory-first coffee journal/inventory, recipe/timer assistants, Notion/physical substitutes, and discovery-first tools.
- Closest direct threats: BeanBook and 빈로그.
- Power-user benchmark: Beanconqueror.
- Guided brew competitors: Filtru and Aeromatic.
- Physical/digital inventory competitor: Coffee Library.
- Korean-first relevant competitors: 빈로그 and DripHub.
- Main differentiation gap: a warm Korean-first memory product that unifies cafe visits, bean logs, brew logs, purchase source, flavor evolution, and "what did this cup mean?" without Beanconqueror-like complexity.

## Notable Competitor Evidence

- Beanconqueror: free/open-source, offline/local-first, very deep parameters and device integrations; risk is complexity and sync friction.
- Filtru: polished guided brewing, IAP/subscription visible; risk is bugs/ads/guide coverage complaints.
- Aeromatic: excellent AeroPress recipe assistant; too narrow for memory.
- BeanBook: AI bag scan, coffee shelf, discovery, Pro tier; closest all-in-one threat.
- Coffee Library: Apple Intelligence autofill, NFC/QR, resting reminders; strong inventory angle.
- 빈로그: brew logs, recipe duplication, explore mode, cafe logs, SCA flavor-wheel profile, freshness, cloud backup, share cards; closest Korean-first memory/discovery threat.
- DripHub: Korean coffee diary with beans, recipes, gear, and tasting notes; public footprint thin.
- Notion/physical journals: flexible/fast but no native coffee semantics, search, trends, AI, or discovery.

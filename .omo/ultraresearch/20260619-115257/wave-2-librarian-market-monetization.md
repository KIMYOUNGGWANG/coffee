## Key Findings

- Best single Korea market source is aT/FIS 2024 coffee market report: it covers supply, production/import-export, distribution, consumer trends, and overseas market sections.
- Korea is a high-frequency coffee market: sourced reports cite 405 cups per person per year in 2023.
- Storefront market is saturated and mature; KOSIS/franchise/news evidence points to very large coffee-shop scale.
- Home-cafe and roasted-bean growth support bean-level memory, roast-profile tracking, and home-brewing workflows.
- Specialty coffee repurchase is tied to benefit seeking, identity, and service differentiation, not simply low price.
- Price sensitivity is real; expected coffee prices can diverge from actual prices, and low-cost-chain users cite reasonable price as a key selection reason.
- Subscription familiarity is high in Korea, but app/in-app pricing must be transparent, cancellable, and easy to understand.

## Monetization Implications

- Best-supported wedge: premium coffee memory + decision support.
- Free tier: taste log, bean history, favorite cafes, basic reminders.
- Paid tier: AI taste memory, origin/roast recommendation, purchase optimization, recurring bean/cafe tracking, exportable preference profile.
- Later commerce take-rate: bean links, cafe partnerships, subscription-box referral, member-only drops.
- Value framing should emphasize remembering what users liked, avoiding bad purchases, building a personal bean/taste profile, and helping home-cafe users choose better faster.
